# Illustration Guide

**Status:** Approved  
**Owner:** Founder & Product Advisor  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-15

---

# Purpose

DailyPaws illustrations are not decorative artwork.

They are part of the product experience.

Their purpose is to create emotional warmth, reinforce the feeling of discovery, and make the product feel calm, friendly and approachable.

Every illustration should support the feeling that DailyPaws is about building a relationship with your dog through small everyday moments.

---

# Design Philosophy

DailyPaws is not about dog training.

It is about curiosity.

Illustrations should communicate:

- shared experiences
- exploration
- calm moments
- trust
- companionship
- joy
- discovery

They should never communicate:

- obedience
- competition
- achievement
- performance
- perfection

---

# Brand Personality

Illustrations should feel:

- Warm
- Calm
- Friendly
- Scandinavian
- Timeless
- Thoughtful
- Playful (without being childish)
- Optimistic
- Human

The overall feeling should be:

> "I want to spend more time with my dog."

---

# Illustration Principles

Illustrations should communicate emotion rather than instructions.

They should capture moments rather than actions.

Whenever possible, illustrate:

- connection
- curiosity
- attention
- exploration
- calmness

Instead of:

- commands
- training exercises
- equipment
- technical detail

---

# Characters

Illustrations should feature ordinary people.

The focus is not on age, gender or fashion.

Instead they should feel:

- relatable
- diverse
- relaxed
- approachable

Dogs should also feel like individuals.

Avoid exaggerating breed-specific traits.

Illustrations should work equally well whether the user's dog is a Labrador, Labradoodle, Dachshund or mixed breed.

---

# Dog Behaviour

Dogs should look natural.

Avoid exaggerated expressions.

Preferred behaviours include:

- sniffing
- looking around
- resting
- exploring
- making eye contact
- walking beside people
- observing

Avoid:

- aggressive behaviour
- exaggerated excitement
- cartoon reactions
- unrealistic poses

---

# Colour Palette

Use warm, muted colours.

Inspired by:

- Scandinavian interiors
- nature
- autumn forests
- soft textiles
- warm afternoon light

Avoid:

- saturated colours
- neon
- harsh contrasts
- overly colourful scenes

The interface should remain the primary source of colour.

Illustrations support the UI rather than dominate it.

---

# Style

Preferred style:

- Hand-drawn
- Soft vector illustration
- Minimal detail
- Thin outlines
- Rounded shapes
- Gentle shadows
- Clean white background
- Plenty of whitespace

Illustrations should feel modern without becoming trendy.

---

# Composition

Illustrations should be simple.

Usually include:

- one dog
- one or two people
- one environment

Avoid crowded scenes.

The user's attention should immediately understand the emotion being communicated.

---

# Environment

Illustrations should reflect everyday life.

Examples:

- home
- garden
- park
- forest
- café
- path
- lakeshore
- city street

Nature should appear regularly.

---

# Mission Illustrations

Mission illustrations should represent the feeling of a Mission, not the exact activity.

Example:

Mission:

Walk your usual route in the opposite direction.

Illustration:

A person and dog happily exploring together.

Not:

A map showing arrows.

---

# Insight Illustrations

Insights should communicate reflection.

Good examples:

- sitting together
- looking at each other
- resting
- quiet moments
- shared success

Insights should never feel like rewards or achievements.

---

# Timeline Illustrations

Timeline illustrations should evoke memories.

Think:

"We remember this day."

Not:

"We completed this task."

---

# Empty States

Empty states should feel hopeful.

Illustrations should invite the user forward rather than remind them something is missing.

---

# Illustration System

Version 1 will contain reusable illustrations for:

- Home
- Observe Missions
- Practice Missions
- Explore Missions
- Celebrate Missions
- Relationship Insights
- Dog Insights
- Owner Insights
- Timeline Empty State
- Profile
- General Empty State

Mission-specific illustrations may be introduced in future versions.

---

# Future Vision

Over time, illustrations should help tell the story of the relationship.

The visual language should become an important part of DailyPaws' identity.

The goal is for users to recognise a DailyPaws illustration instantly, even without seeing the logo.

---

# Design Principle

Illustrations should communicate emotion,
not instruction.

They should help users feel something before they read anything.

If an illustration makes someone smile, slow down or want to spend time with their dog, it has succeeded.

# Inspiration

DailyPaws illustrations should feel closer to a beautifully illustrated children's book than to a technical mobile app.

The goal is timeless warmth rather than visual novelty.
