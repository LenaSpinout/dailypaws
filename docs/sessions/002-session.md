---

# Goal

Design the onboarding experience and define how DailyPaws gets to know the dog and the family.

---

# Biggest insights

## Onboarding should be short

The initial onboarding should take around 5 minutes.

The goal is not to fully understand the dog.

The goal is to know enough to create the first meaningful mission.

---

## DailyPaws learns over time

Instead of asking everything during onboarding, DailyPaws gradually learns about the dog and the family's relationships through conversations.

Every mission is an opportunity to learn something new.

---

## The first assessment has two separate parts

### Part 1

Who is your dog?

This focuses on:

- personality
- motivations
- challenges
- everyday life
- strengths
- current goals

---

### Part 2

Different people.
Different relationships.

The relationship between the dog and every family member is described separately.

Relationships are never good or bad.

They are unique.

---

## Assessment becomes conversation

Instead of asking random questions later, DailyPaws asks relevant follow-up questions based on previous answers and completed missions.

Example:

"We noticed you're working on biting..."

"When does it usually happen?"

---

## AI should create insights

The purpose of AI is not primarily to generate training plans.

Its biggest value is helping the family understand their dog.

Examples:

"I think Bellman is unusually motivated by..."

"Many dogs struggle with this. Bellman already does it very well."

"I may be wrong, but it seems that..."

The AI should always sound humble.

It should invite confirmation rather than present facts.

---

## End of onboarding

Instead of saying:

"Assessment complete"

DailyPaws presents:

# Meet Bellman

A warm summary describing the dog's personality, strengths and areas for growth.

The user can react:

👍 That's Bellman!

🤔 Mostly

👎 Not really

The summary becomes the starting point for DailyPaws.

---

# Biggest Product Insight

DailyPaws should make users feel understood before trying to teach them anything.

Trust comes before coaching.
