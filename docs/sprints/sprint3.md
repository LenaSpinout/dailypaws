# Sprint 3 – A Week Together

## Theme

A family should want to come back tomorrow.

Sprint 2 proved that DailyPaws can remember.

Sprint 3 is about making the experience feel rich, varied and enjoyable enough that a family naturally wants to continue using it for at least one week.

This sprint focuses on improving the product experience rather than introducing new intelligence.

---

# Sprint Goal

Make DailyPaws engaging enough for one week of real family testing.

---

# Success Criteria

By the end of Sprint 3:

✅ Mission library feels varied and fresh.

✅ Home feels alive and communicates progress.

✅ The learning journey feels continuous over several days.

✅ The product is polished enough for external family testing.

---

Included

---

# Product Principles

During Sprint 3 we optimise for:

- Variety
- Curiosity
- Delight
- Calmness
- Simplicity

We do not optimise for AI sophistication.

---

# Risks

The biggest product risk is not technical.

The biggest risk is that families lose interest because the experience feels repetitive.

Every implementation during this sprint should therefore ask:

"Does this make someone want to come back tomorrow?"

---

# Definition of Success

At the end of Sprint 3, DailyPaws should feel like a product that a real family could happily use for a week.

That—not feature count—is the measure of success.
