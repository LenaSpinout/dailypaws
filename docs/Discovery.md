# Discovery 1

This document contains ideas, hypotheses and concepts that are currently being explored.

Nothing in this document is considered a product decision until it has been moved into the Product Bible or Product Decisions.

---

# Product Vision

DailyPaws is not just another dog training app.

It is a relationship app for families living with dogs.

The app should help people notice progress, strengthen relationships and enjoy everyday life together.

---

# The Learning Journey

DailyPaws should not try to learn everything during onboarding.

Instead it should get to know the family gradually.

The app should feel like an experienced dog trainer who becomes wiser after every interaction.

Every mission teaches something.

Every answer teaches something.

Every observation teaches something.

---

# Initial Onboarding

Target duration: approximately 5 minutes.

The goal is not to fully understand the dog.

The goal is to gather enough information to create the first meaningful mission.

The onboarding should feel like a conversation rather than a questionnaire.

---

# Continuous Learning

After onboarding, DailyPaws keeps learning.

Instead of asking random questions, the app asks contextual follow-up questions based on:

- previous answers

- completed missions

- observed progress

- current goals

Examples:

"We noticed you're working on biting."

"When does it usually happen?"

---

"I think Bellman enjoys nose work."

"Does that sound right?"

---

"I'm curious..."

"When does Bellman seem most relaxed?"

---

# Two Different Models

The app should model two different things.

## 1. Who is your dog?

This describes the dog itself.

Possible areas:

- Personality

- Motivations

- Strengths

- Challenges

- Everyday life

- Skills

- Current goals

---

## 2. Different people. Different relationships.

Every relationship between the dog and a family member is unique.

The app should understand questions such as:

- Who does Bellman sleep next to?

- Who does Bellman bring toys to?

- Who does Bellman listen to best?

- Who does Bellman feel safest with?

- Who plays the wildest games?

- Who trims the nails?

- Who brushes the coat?

- Who does Bellman follow around the house?

- Who does Bellman greet first?

- Who does Bellman wrestle with?

- Who does Bellman seek when he feels uncertain?

Relationships are not judged.

They are simply understood.

---

# Personality Insights

Instead of presenting scores, DailyPaws should summarize what it believes it has learned.

Examples:

"I think Bellman is especially motivated by..."

"It seems that..."

"I may be wrong, but..."

"Many dogs struggle with..."

"Unlike many dogs..."

"Bellman seems unusually good at..."

The user should always be able to confirm or correct the summary.

---

# Meet Your Dog

At the end of onboarding, DailyPaws presents a warm personality summary.

Not an assessment.

Not statistics.

A story.

Example:

"Meet Bellman."

"I think I've started to get to know him."

The summary should describe:

- personality

- motivations

- strengths

- current challenges

- first recommendations

The user responds:

👍 That's Bellman!

🤔 Mostly

👎 Not really

---

# The Conversation Engine

The app should communicate like an encouraging coach.

Examples:

"I noticed..."

"I'm curious..."

"I think..."

"I may be wrong..."

"Can I ask you something?"

Questions should never feel like homework.

---

# The Insight Engine

AI should primarily create understanding.

Not training plans.

Examples of future insights:

"Bellman seems to learn faster when missions are short."

"It looks like tug games are becoming more rewarding than food."

"Compared with two months ago, Bellman seems much calmer around small dogs."

The purpose is to help families notice progress.

---

# Moments

The app should encourage families to capture meaningful moments.

Examples:

"The first calm dog meeting."

"Bellman chose to look at you instead of the ball."

"He settled by himself at the café."

Moments are memories.

Not statistics.

- How should personality summaries evolve over time?

# Family

Ideas

- A family consists of people.
- Every person has one or more roles.
- Every person has one unique relationship with the dog.
- Roles can change over time.
- Relationships evolve independently.
- Every relationship has a unique superpower.
