# Mission Ideas – Brainstorm

Raw pool of Mission ideas for Issue #20. Quantity and variety
over polish. Best ideas will be curated into MissionCatalog.md.
Format: [Mission Type] Idea. Grouped by Goal Type.

## Everyday Life

1. [Practice] Swap who does one daily routine today — if one
   person always feeds, someone else does it. Notice what the
   dog does differently.
2. [Explore] Take your usual walk in the opposite direction.
   Same route, reversed — watch where the dog hesitates or
   speeds up.
3. [Observe] During dinner prep, give the dog nothing to do —
   just notice where they choose to be and what they watch.
4. [Practice] Let the dog choose at every corner on today's
   walk. Follow their nose completely for ten minutes.
5. [Celebrate] Recreate the dog's favourite part of the day —
   but do it twice today.
6. [Practice] Change one small thing in the home (move the
   water bowl, open a door that's usually closed) and watch
   how the dog handles the change.
7. [Explore] Do a "slow walk": take five minutes to cover the
   first hundred meters. Let the dog sniff everything fully.
8. [Observe] Sit on the floor at dog level for ten minutes
   during an ordinary evening. See what the dog does with the
   invitation.
9. [Practice] Rainy day: put on rain gear and go out anyway,
   just five minutes. Notice whether the weather bothers the
   dog or mostly you.
10. [Celebrate] End the day with a two-minute calm moment in
    the dog's favourite spot — no phone, just presence.
11. [Practice] Give the dog a job during a routine: carry
    something small on the walk, or "help" bring in the mail.
12. [Explore] Visit a place you pass daily but never stop at —
    the bench, the courtyard, the parking lot with good smells.

## Adventure

13. [Explore] Take the car somewhere ten minutes away you've
    never walked. No goal — just twenty minutes of new ground.
14. [Explore] Forest mission: find a fallen tree and let the
    dog investigate it fully. Climb, sniff, balance if they want.
15. [Practice] Pack a "dog picnic" — water, a treat, a blanket —
    and walk until you find the right spot together.
16. [Explore] Walk in light rain or wind on purpose. New
    weather changes every smell — watch the dog read the world.
17. [Explore] Take a bus or tram one stop with the dog (where
    allowed) and walk home. A tiny expedition.
18. [Celebrate] Return to the place of a great memory — the
    beach, the meadow — and let the dog lead the visit.
19. [Explore] Night walk with a headlamp somewhere familiar.
    Darkness turns a known route into new territory.
20. [Practice] Let the dog pick the trail at every fork in the
    forest. You're the companion today, not the guide.
21. [Explore] Find water — a stream, a puddle, a lakeshore —
    and give the dog ten unhurried minutes with it.
22. [Practice] Build a tiny "expedition kit" together and take
    it on a walk: the dog sniffs each item before it's packed.
23. [Explore] Urban adventure: walk through a part of town with
    completely different sounds — market, harbour, station square.
24. [Celebrate] Summit something small — a hill, a lookout —
    and share a snack at the top like proper adventurers.

## Activity

25. [Practice] Hide three treats in the living room while the
    dog waits in another room. Watch their search strategy.
26. [Practice] Build a mini obstacle in the hallway from
    cushions and a broomstick. Let the dog solve it their way.
27. [Practice] Play "which hand?" with a treat five times.
    Notice how the dog figures the game out.
28. [Explore] Take the dog's favourite game outdoors — or an
    outdoor game indoors — and see how it changes.
29. [Practice] Scatter the dog's dinner in the grass instead of
    the bowl (weather permitting). Dinner becomes a treasure hunt.
30. [Practice] Introduce a cardboard box with treats hidden in
    crumpled paper. Recycling becomes enrichment.
31. [Celebrate] Ten minutes of the dog's absolute favourite
    game, ending while it's still fun.
32. [Practice] Try a "sniffari": a walk where the only purpose
    is smells. The dog sets the pace and route entirely.
33. [Practice] Teach yourselves a new game (not a command):
    tug with rules, find-the-person, follow-the-leader.
34. [Explore] Park mission: find three things the dog can
    safely climb on, go under, or balance across.
35. [Practice] Frozen treat experiment: freeze something tasty
    in ice and let the dog work it out on the balcony or lawn.
36. [Celebrate] Film thirty seconds of your favourite shared
    activity and watch it together tonight.

## Behaviour

37. [Observe] On today's walk, count what the dog notices
    before you do. Just tally — dogs, bikes, sounds, smells.
38. [Practice] When visitors arrive today, give the dog a
    lick mat in their spot beforehand. Watch what changes.
39. [Practice] Car trip: make the boarding routine extra calm
    and slow, then notice how the ride itself goes.
40. [Observe] Find the dog's "threshold distance" to something
    exciting — how close before they can't look away?
41. [Practice] In a busy place, find a calm bench and just sit
    together for five minutes, letting the world pass by.
42. [Practice] Practice "boring departures": pick up your keys,
    put on shoes — then sit back down. Watch the reaction fade.
43. [Observe] Notice what the dog does in the minute before
    something they find difficult. What are the early signals?
44. [Practice] Change one thing about a hard situation —
    approach the dog park from the other side — and compare.
45. [Practice] Let the dog watch the vacuum cleaner while it's
    off, with treats nearby. No pressure, just presence.
46. [Observe] During a family visit, track where the dog
    chooses to be over one hour. Map their comfort zones.
47. [Practice] On walks today, reward every time the dog checks
    in with you voluntarily — no cue, just noticing you.
48. [Practice] Doorbell experiment: ring it yourself while
    everyone's home and calm. See what half the trigger does.

## Connection

49. [Celebrate] Give the dog a full-body slow massage tonight
    and learn where they melt and where they'd rather not.
50. [Observe] Spend five minutes copying your dog: lie where
    they lie, look where they look. See the room their way.
51. [Practice] Have the quietest family member do today's walk
    alone with the dog. One-on-one time changes things.
52. [Celebrate] Make a "favourites day": favourite route,
    favourite game, favourite treat, favourite person's lap.
53. [Practice] Talk to your dog during the whole walk today —
    narrate, chat, sing. Notice the ear movements.
54. [Observe] Watch your dog sleep for five minutes. Dream
    twitches, position, where they chose to sleep — near whom?
55. [Practice] Teach the dog each family member's name by
    saying it and having that person call. A family game.
56. [Celebrate] Let the dog unwrap something: a treat loosely
    rolled in a towel. Celebrate the small victory together.
57. [Practice] Do "nothing" together in the garden or on a
    blanket for ten minutes. Boredom is where bonds show up.
58. [Observe] Notice who the dog greets first when the family
    comes home — and how differently they greet each person.
59. [Practice] Car trip to nowhere: drive ten minutes, share a
    snack in the car, drive home. The trip is the point.
60. [Celebrate] Create a family "hall of fame" moment: recreate
    the dog's funniest habit and everyone applauds.
